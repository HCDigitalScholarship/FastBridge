Formulas Explanation

Here you will find the explanations for the formulas used in our metrics.

Metrics

Word: For the purposes of these analyses, proper names (e.g., Marcus, Roma) are excluded from the calculations.
Number of Words: The total number of lemma in the text (i.e., “tokens” or N).
Vocabulary or Unique Words: The number of different words that appear in the text (i.e., “types” or V). All inflected forms of a word are considered as that word (e.g., “amico” and “amici”, or “agis” and “egerunt”) would count as a single type (“amicus” or “ago”).
LexR: A measurement of lexical readability on a 10-point scale for historical languages that blends data about word length, lexical high frequency, lexical sophistication, and lexical variation.
PLexR: PLexR calculates a text’s readability by replacing LexR’s generic parameters for lexical high frequency and lexical sophistication with customized core and non-core vocabularies.
Lexical Density: The number of lexical or content words (nouns, adjectives, verbs, and adverbs) divided by the total number of words in a text.
Lexical Sophistication: The number of unusual or advanced words in the text, defined as those words that are not in the Diederich 1500.
Lexical Variation: How often individual words are repeated within a text can also contribute to its lexical complexity. Because this metric is biased.
CTTR or Corrected TTR: Corrected TTR divides the number of types (V) by the square-root of 2 times the number of tokens (N).
Root TTR: Root TTR divides the number of types (V) by the square-root of the number tokens.
LogTTR or Herdan’s C: LogTTR divides the log of the number of types (V) by the log of the number of tokens (N).
TTR: The ratio of the number of unique words (“types”) to the total number of words (“tokens”). This formula exhibits bias against shorter texts.

Plots

Word Frequency: Plots a histogram of the most common words found in text selection, no exclusions.
Cumulative Lexical Load: Assigns a lexical score to each word of the text, displays the ever-growing cumulative difficulty/load of the text.
Linear Lexical Load: Assigns a lexical score to each word, displays the change in lexical load of the text selection.
Bin Frequency: Displays the percentage of words in text selection that fall into certain frequency bins within the Diederich 1500.

Hapax

Hapax (legomenon): A word that only appears once in the selection.
